# MSDS-COMP-GH: Components G and H — Liquid Reaction Products

**Identity:** Components G and H, the two liquid products of the reactor's primary exothermic reactions (reactions producing G consume A, C, D; reactions producing H consume A, C, E), separated from the stripper liquid product stream (stream 11) and reported as product composition (XMEAS 37-41).

**Physical state:** Liquid at process conditions.

## Hazard summary

- **Flammability:** Combustible liquid. Lower volatility than the feed gases, but hot process-temperature liquid (product stream leaves the stripper near stripper temperature, XMEAS 18) presents a splash/contact burn hazard in addition to a combustible-liquid hazard if released.
- **Reactivity:** Stable under normal storage conditions once separated from the reactor. Not known to react hazardously with Components G or H against each other; the reactivity hazard in this process is upstream, in the reactor itself, not in the separated product.
- **Thermal hazard:** Product leaves the stripper at an elevated temperature; unplanned contact with skin or incompatible materials at that temperature is a burn/ignition hazard independent of the product's chemical hazard profile.
- **Ratio monitoring note:** The G:H product ratio is not itself a safety-critical variable in isolation, but per the reactor_kinetics_drift near-miss incident, a sustained, unexplained drift in this ratio — absent any corresponding feed or setpoint change — is a leading indicator of a reaction kinetics deviation upstream in the reactor, and should be escalated per SOP-REACT-001 even when no downstream reading involving G or H itself is out of range.

## First response (non-exhaustive; defer to full plant emergency procedures for actual response)

Treat any unplanned product-stream release as a hot-liquid hazard first (burn risk) and a combustible-liquid hazard second; do not use water for direct contact suppression without confirming compatibility.
